Hotshot Trucking: The Big 3 Dually Showdown

RAM 3500 vs Ford F-450 vs Chevy Silverado 3500HD — which rig actually earns its keep on the road?

Hotshot trucking isn't glamorous — it's early mornings, tight deadlines, and a whole lot of miles. Your truck is your livelihood, and picking the wrong one costs you money. We stacked up the Big 3 heavy-duty duallies side by side so you don't have to guess.

🚛 RAM 3500
RAM 3500 Laramie
★★★★★

RAM 3500 — The Workhorse King

Best-in-class torque. Built for operators who need numbers, not just a name on the tailgate.

  • Engine 6.7L Cummins Turbo Diesel
  • Max Torque 1,075 lb-ft
  • GVWR 14,000 lbs
  • Max Tow 37,100 lbs
  • Payload 7,680 lbs
Pros
  • Highest torque in class — hill climbs feel effortless
  • Exceptional ride comfort with coil-spring rear
  • Air suspension available for leveling under load
  • Interior quality punches above its price point
Cons
  • Slightly lower max tow rating than F-450
  • DEF/emissions maintenance adds operating cost
  • Cummins long-term reliability varies by mileage
Hotshot Take: The RAM 3500 is the driver's choice. That Cummins torque makes loaded mountain grades feel like flat highway. If you're running livestock or heavy steel, this is your truck.
This Week’s Pick
🚛 F-450
Ford F-450 Super Duty
★★★★★

Ford F-450 — The Maximum Load Master

Class-leading tow and payload ratings. When the load spec says you need every pound, the F-450 delivers.

  • Engine 6.7L Power Stroke Diesel
  • Max Torque 1,050 lb-ft
  • GVWR 14,000 lbs
  • Max Tow 40,000 lbs
  • Payload 8,000 lbs
Pros
  • Highest max tow rating of any pickup — period
  • Best-in-class payload capacity
  • Proven Power Stroke reliability across high mileage
  • Pro Trailer Backup Assist is genuinely useful
Cons
  • Ride is stiff unloaded — leaf springs feel every bump
  • Cab interior lags behind RAM on premium feel
  • Turning radius requires patience in tight lots
Hotshot Take: If your business lives and dies on max payload and DOT compliance, the F-450 is the answer. That 40,000 lb tow rating isn't marketing — it's real-world engineering margin that protects your livelihood.
🚛 Silverado 3500HD
Chevy Silverado 3500HD
★★★★☆

Silverado 3500HD — The Value Play

Strong numbers, lower entry price, and the Duramax diesel still earns its reputation on long hauls.

  • Engine 6.6L Duramax Turbo Diesel
  • Max Torque 975 lb-ft
  • GVWR 14,000 lbs
  • Max Tow 36,000 lbs
  • Payload 7,442 lbs
Pros
  • Best price-to-capability ratio of the three
  • Duramax/Allison combo is legendary for durability
  • Best fuel economy of the diesel trio
  • Wide dealer network = easier service nationwide
Cons
  • Lowest torque rating in this comparison
  • Interior feels a step behind RAM at similar trims
  • Tow rating falls short of F-450 for max-spec loads
Hotshot Take: The Silverado 3500HD is the smart buy for new hotshot operators watching startup costs. The Duramax-Allison pairing is bulletproof, and you'll spend less time at the dealer than you might with the competition.
